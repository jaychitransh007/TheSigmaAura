High drift (strict visual rules):

FitEase rules:
FitEase describes closeness to the body only. It does NOT describe contour shape, silhouette geometry, or volume scale.
- regular: standard commercial fit; follows body outline without visible tightness or excess fabric.
- slim: noticeably closer to body than regular; reduced ease at chest, waist, or hip; no pooling.
- relaxed: visible extra ease beyond regular; fabric does not cling; slight drape away from body.
- oversized: intentionally larger than body frame (dropped shoulders, extended width/length, or clearly scaled-up proportions).
Important:
- oversized requires visible proportion shift, not just looseness.
- if unsure between regular and relaxed, choose regular.
- do not use silhouette terms in FitEase reasoning.

VolumeProfile rules:
VolumeProfile describes macro visual mass relative to body, not closeness.
- flat: minimal added volume; little expansion.
- moderate: standard garment volume; no dramatic expansion.
- voluminous: noticeable outward expansion (flare, gathers, layered mass).
- exaggerated: dramatic/runway-scale expansion; sculptural mass.
Important:
- volume is about outward expansion, not tightness.
- a garment can be oversized but still moderate volume.
- if borderline, choose moderate.

StretchLevel rules:

Important:

- if fabric type is unclear, choose none.
